Classes

Vocal

We take classes for traditional Carnatic vocal as well as for other branches of music such as bhajans, slokas, light music and devotional music. We also take specialized sloka classes for certain stream of slokas such as Bhagavat Gita, Lalitha Sahasra Nama, Daily chanting slokas etc. Classes are conducted by expert musicians on weekdays as well as on weekends. Students of any age group and category are welcome to learn the wonderful world of music

Violin

Classes are conducted for students of all age groups on learning Carnatic Violin. Our approach to learn violin is through music. Students will be thought a piece of music lesson and the corresponding lesson will be thought on violin. Violin classes are conducted by professional violinists.

Kathak

Kathakali is a complete art – combination of Nrittam, Nrityam and Natyam. We take classes for a person to learn kathakali to perform on stage as well as for a person who is interested in learning the art’s basics so that they can appreciate this art when they watch. Classes are conducted at present for Kathakali on every Sunday. Classes are conducted by professional artists. There is no bar on age, gender etc for learning Kathakali.

About Us



  • 2008-2011

    Our Humble Beginnings

    Started Our Institution Roots in Singapore
    Our Founder and Chief Artistic Director
    Dr. Ganesh Neelakanata Iyer has conducted several workshops and performed at Singapore, Malaysia and Indonesia. He has performed infront of many dignitaries including the then Singapore President

  • 2012

    A New Milestone

    Our Co-Founder, Chief Executive Officer and Chief Music Director Mrs Lakshmy Ganesh worked with Padmabhushan Dr L Subramaniam and performed violin along with Mr Ambi Subrahmaniam in NUS Arts Festival Singapore on songs composed by Dr L Subramaniam

  • 2013 - 2017

    Welcoming Foundations

    We established Sabarish School of Arts and Music in Lingampally, Hyderabad We introduced the wonderful world of music and arts to several students in Hyderabad. We used to take regualr classes in Hyderabad and performed for several times in Sri Venkateswara Temple, BHEL. Kathakali has been performed for several times in many parts of Hyderabad. We have also conducted workshops on Katahakli at schools and other public venues.

  • 2015

    Phase Two Expansion

    A key year for our institution Our Founder and Chief Artistic Director Dr Ganesh Neelakanata Iyer has written a new Kathakali story (Aattakatha) on Sri Mookambika Mahathmyam and had its debut performance in his home town Thodupuzha

  • 2017

    Welcoming Foundations

    Our Founder and Chief Artistic Director Dr Ganesh Neelakanata Iyer along with senior artists from Kerala performed Sri Mookambika Mahathmyam at Kollur Mookambika, Karnataka

  • 2018 - Now

    Phase Two Expansion

    We have moved our institution to Coimbatore We have renamed our institution name to "Sabarish School of Performing Arts" Regular classes on Vocal, Violin and Kathakali are going on We are activley performing at different venues

Instructors

Lakshmy

Vocal and Violin

Mrs Lakshmy Ganesh

Co-Founder, Chief Executive Officer and Chief Music Director

Lakshmy Ganesh (Professional Vocalist and Professional Violinist) is a disciple of Vadyarathnam Sri Kalanjoor D Vishwanathan who is a disciple of Sangeetha Kalanithi Sri M Chandrasekharan. She ahs more than 15 years of experience in performing Violin and Vocal in different parts of India. She also performed Violin with Padmabhushan Dr L Subramaniam and Ambi Subramaniam at NUS Arts Festival Singapore. She is an exponent in teaching both Violin and Vocal for students of all ages.

Ganesh Iyer

Kathakali

Dr Ganesh Neelakanta Iyer

Founder and Chief Artistic Director

Dr Ganesh Neelakanta Iyer started learning Kathakali at the age of 12. He has performed Kathakali and conducted several interactive workshops on Kathakali and other classical arts for different participants at various countries including Singapore, Malaysia and Indonesia. He has learnt Carnatic Music for more than 15 years and has learnt several other art forms including Violin, Mridangam and Therukkoothu. He has written a Kathakali story about Lord Mookambika – Sri Mookambika Mahathmyam which was staged in several places in India. He also holds a PhD in Computer Science.
